Accountant

An accountant is someone who works with auditing. The purpose of auditing is an independent review and control of a company's accounts. The certified public accountant is trained and specialized in working to give the company and the company's stakeholders an insight into the company's financial conditions on a neutral and objective basis. The auditor is also obliged to follow laws and regulations when reviewing a company's finances, and with this to ensure that a company's accounts provide an auditing picture of the company's financial condition.

The different kinds of accountants

There are several different types of auditors. Today, anyone can call themselves an accountant, as the title of auditor is not protected. This means that an accountant by name does not necessarily have an education that equips the person in question for auditing work, but is, for example, self-taught through experience in accounting.

It is therefore important that, when choosing an auditor, you consider whether the auditor is equipped to take care of a company's interests with the right professional insight and professionalism, which you can certainly expect from an approved auditor.

If you want to get an approved auditor, where you are sure that he or she has the required knowledge and insight to handle a company's financial matters, you are advised to look for the two auditors who have been granted an authorization scheme for their training. accountant-acquired. They are per education qualified to handle a number of auditing tasks, as they have acquired very specific auditing skills with their educations.

These auditors are;

The state-authorized public accountant (cand.merc.aud.) The path to the title of state-certified public accountant can get through two possible educational paths.

Office training (specialty auditing) with internship in an auditing firm and then HD 1st part and HD 2nd part, or

A high school education followed by an HA bachelor's degree at a business school

The above opens up the possibility of taking a university degree called cand.merc.aud. Master's education must be followed by 2-4 years of internship in auditing. The auditor can then be nominated for the examination as a state-authorized public accountant.

The Registered Accountant The registered auditor is very similar to the certified public accountant. This is also subject to the same laws and regulations as the latter. The registered auditor has completed a two-year basic course in the master's program cand.merc.aud., In contrast to the state-authorized public accountant. Thereafter, the registered auditor has also undergone an internship of 2-3 years at an auditing firm and completed the training with a qualification examination, while a state-authorized public accountant has taken an auditing examination.

The future belongs to the statutory auditor

The training as a registered auditor has been discontinued. In future, there will only be one type of certified public accountant, which is the state-authorized public accountant. The final qualification exams for the registered auditor will be held during 2017-2019.

The auditor's duties

The approved accountant is of great importance to society. In addition to preparing and approving financial statements and advising companies and individuals on their financial matters, the auditor is also a guarantor of compliance with applicable laws and standards.

In addition to the above, the statutory auditor is also qualified to perform the following tasks:

Audit and control of companies' accounts,

Provide accounting and tax assistance,

Company assessment in connection with purchase, sale, merger or division,

Helping a company with a group structure,

Listing,

Generational change.

In addition, the state-authorized or registered auditor also has knowledge of pension schemes, accounting systems and grant options.

The Certified Public Accountant - the right auditor

The right accountant can therefore be useful for the individual, the small as well as the large company and can be the security for optimal financial and tax solutions and advice.

It is advisable to enter into a collaboration with an approved auditor - either the registered auditor or the state-authorized public accountant - as they are qualified to perform very specific tasks that many do not have the required knowledge of. Furthermore, due to their authorization, they are subject to the auditing legislation which requires them to quality control their work and live up to the documentation obligation, which is why their customer is assured of errors and omissions, should this be the case.
